Commercial Electrical Shutdown Planning

Coordinate people, equipment, access, communications, and restart responsibilities before power is interrupted.

Identify affected operations

List occupants, tenants, production equipment, refrigeration, alarms, access control, communications, servers, lighting, elevators, and mechanical systems that may be affected. Review the scope with a provider of commercial electrical services.

Assign responsibilities

  • Name the person who approves the shutdown and the person who communicates with occupants.
  • Confirm who safely stops and restarts specialized equipment.
  • Arrange access to electrical rooms, tenant spaces, roofs, and locked service areas.
  • Keep emergency contacts and escalation instructions available.

Coordinate the electrical scope

Include planned panel work, lighting upgrades, and maintenance items in one written schedule so dependencies are visible.

Restart note: A shutdown plan should include a controlled restart sequence and a method for recording systems that do not return to normal operation.

Prepare the service request

Send the operating schedule, affected systems, access constraints, and site contact through the BGE contact page before the proposed work window.
