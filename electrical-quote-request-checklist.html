What to include in an electrical quote request

A useful quote request explains the desired result, the existing conditions, and the access constraints without guessing at the technical solution. The following checklist helps homeowners and property managers prepare information before an electrical contractor visits.

Describe the outcome first

Start with what needs to work when the project is finished. Examples include adding receptacles to a finished room, supplying a new appliance, improving exterior lighting, preparing a garage for vehicle charging, or reviewing an older panel before a renovation. If the request concerns a fault, describe what you observed, when it happens, and whether the issue is limited to one room or circuit.

Avoid diagnosing the cause yourself. Statements such as “the breaker trips after the heater runs for ten minutes” are more useful than assuming that a panel replacement is required. The electrician can evaluate the circuit, connected equipment, installation conditions, and available capacity.

Prepare a short property summary

  • Property type and approximate age.
  • Whether the space is occupied, rented, or under renovation.
  • Location of the electrical panel and any access limitations.
  • Known additions, renovations, or previous electrical upgrades.
  • Timing requirements and periods when power cannot be interrupted.

For commercial spaces, also identify business hours, landlord or property-manager contacts, and any building rules that affect access or shutdowns.

Photograph only what is safe to reach

Useful photos may show the panel location with its cover closed, the work area from several angles, the equipment nameplate, the route between the panel and proposed load, and any visible damage from a safe distance. Do not remove electrical covers, open service equipment, or touch damaged components to obtain a better image.

List equipment details

For appliances, chargers, heating equipment, pumps, tools, or specialty systems, record the manufacturer and model number. Product instructions often identify voltage, current, circuit, disconnect, and environmental requirements. Sharing the exact model prevents a quote from being based on a different equipment size.

Compare scope, not just the total

Ask what labour, materials, permits, inspections, utility coordination, shutdown time, and finish repairs are included. Confirm who supplies decorative fixtures and who handles drywall, paint, masonry, landscaping, or network work. A lower price may reflect a narrower scope rather than the same work at a lower cost.

Sparking, smoke, a burning smell, visible heat damage, or an immediate shock risk should not be handled as a routine quotation request. Keep people away and contact emergency services or the appropriate utility when required.
