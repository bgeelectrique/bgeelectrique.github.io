Rental Property Electrical Turnover Record

Organize observations, service requests, and completed electrical work between occupancies.

Record observations without dismantling equipment

Walk through each room and note damaged device plates, loose fixtures, non-working lights, unusual heat or odours, recurring breaker concerns reported by the occupant, and equipment affected by water. Leave electrical equipment closed and arrange qualified assessment where needed.

Keep maintenance history together

Store invoices, equipment manuals, inspection documents, warranties, and updated circuit information with the property record. Schedule unresolved items through electrical safety and maintenance services.

Coordinate planned improvements

If the turnover includes flooring, paint, cabinets, appliances, or layout changes, coordinate the electrical portion with renovation electrical work. For unit and common-area needs, review residential electrical services.

Record note: Distinguish a tenant report, a visible observation, and a qualified diagnosis. They are not the same thing.

Request service with clear details

Prepare the property address, access contact, unit number, urgency, photos, and preferred service window before using the BGE contact page. West Island property managers can also review the local West Island electrician service page.
