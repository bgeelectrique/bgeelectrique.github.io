Older Home Device and Lighting Modernization

Build a room-by-room plan before changing outlets, switches, fixtures, controls, or major electrical loads.

Map needs by room

List where occupants rely on extension cords, where switches are inconvenient, which fixtures are difficult to service, and where new equipment will be used. Discuss the complete list during a residential electrical service assessment.

Separate convenience from warning signs

Cosmetic changes and added convenience are different from recurring trips, warm devices, damaged components, flickering, or burning odours. Put safety concerns first and review them through electrical safety and maintenance services.

Coordinate fixtures and controls

For lighting installation, note the desired use, brightness, colour temperature, dimming, control locations, fixture weight, and ceiling access in each space.

Plan for future loads

Upcoming electric heating, cooking, workshop equipment, or vehicle charging may affect panel capacity planning. It is more useful to assess the full load plan than to consider each device in isolation.

Safety note: Do not remove electrical covers or open the panel to prepare photos. Photograph only what is safely visible.

Local service preparation

Owners can review information for an electrician serving Hudson, an electrician serving Saint-Lazare, or use the BGE contact page when the project spans several rooms.
