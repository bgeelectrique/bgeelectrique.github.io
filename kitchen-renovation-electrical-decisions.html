Kitchen Renovation Electrical Decisions

Coordinate appliances, lighting, receptacles, controls, and cabinet details before finishes are locked in.

Freeze the appliance plan early

Collect model numbers and electrical requirements for the range, wall oven, cooktop, microwave, refrigerator, dishwasher, hood, beverage equipment, and any specialty appliance. A licensed electrician can then identify dedicated circuits, available capacity, and routing constraints before cabinets are manufactured.

Mark every working surface

Use the cabinet drawings to mark counters, islands, sinks, appliance garages, pantry equipment, and seating. Coordinate the electrical scope with the broader renovation and new-construction electrical work so device locations are decided before rough-in.

Build a lighting schedule

Separate ambient, task, accent, and decorative fixtures. Note switching locations, dimming preferences, colour temperature, under-cabinet channels, and access to drivers. Review these choices as part of lighting installation planning.

Confirm service capacity

Changing from gas to electric cooking or adding several high-load appliances can affect the service calculation. Ask whether electrical panel upgrade planning should be included before demolition begins.

Coordination note: Keep cabinet, appliance, plumbing, ventilation, and electrical drawings on the same revision so trades are not working from conflicting layouts.

Prepare the site visit

Homeowners can review residential electrical services and contact BGE about the renovation scope with the current drawings and product specifications ready.
